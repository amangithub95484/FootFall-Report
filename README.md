Executive Summary
Over the analyzed week (Jan 5-11), total footfall decreased by -10.8% year-on-year (from 24,056 in 2025 to 21,454 in 2026). However, the end of the week shows a strong positive momentum. While footfall was lower in the first four days of 2026, it surpassed 2025 levels on the final three days (Jan 9, 10, 11). Furthermore, the Average Value per Footfall increased significantly in 2026, indicating that while fewer customers visited, those who did spent more money.

1. Day-by-Day Footfall Comparison (2025 vs 2026)
The trend shifted dramatically mid-week. In early 2025, footfall started high and dipped mid-week. In 2026, footfall started low but grew consistently, peaking at the end.

Strong Finish: The weekend (Jan 10 & 11) in 2026 saw significantly higher traffic than in 2025, with Jan 10 showing a 26.2% increase.

Higher Spending per Visitor: Despite 2,602 fewer visitors in 2026, the business generated more revenue. The average customer in 2026 spent $3,239 (Net Value), which is 18.2% more than the 2025 average of $2,740.

Efficiency: The increase in GV (+12.3%) and NV (+5.4%) against a drop in footfall suggests higher average transaction values or a shift towards higher-priced items.

3. Conversion & Operational Metrics
Items per Customer: The "Bill Qty" per footfall remained relatively stable (0.51 in 2025 vs. 0.54 in 2026). This suggests the increase in spending is due to higher prices/value per item rather than customers buying more items.

Peak Performance (Jan 11, 2026): The last day of the data (Sunday, Jan 11, 2026) was exceptional. It recorded the highest footfall (4,995), highest Gross Value (24.7M), and highest Net Value (14.9M) across both years. This indicates a highly successful trading day.

Conclusion & Recommendations
Shift in Customer Behavior: There appears to be a shift from "high footfall, low spend" (2025) to "optimized footfall, high spend" (2026). The marketing or sales strategy in 2026 seems to be attracting fewer, but higher-value, customers.

Weekend Strength: The data strongly suggests that the weekend (specifically Jan 10 & 11) is becoming increasingly critical. Consider allocating more resources (staff, stock) to these days in 2026, as they significantly outperformed the previous year.

Investigate the Slow Start: The dramatic drop on Jan 5 and 6, 2026, warrants investigation. Was this due to a holiday difference, bad weather, a promotion in 2025 that wasn't repeated, or a competitor's event?

Monitor the Trend: It will be important to see if the trend of higher-value transactions continues into the following weeks, or if it was specific to this post-holiday period.
